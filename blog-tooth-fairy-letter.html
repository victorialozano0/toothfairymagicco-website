The Ultimate Tooth Fairy Letter Template (Free Printable!)

There is something undeniably magical about the Tooth Fairy. The whispered excitement at bedtime, the careful placement of a tiny tooth under the pillow, the wide-eyed wonder in the morning — it is one of childhood's most enchanting rituals. But what if the Tooth Fairy left more than just a coin? What if she left a letter?

A personalized Tooth Fairy letter transforms an already special moment into something your child will remember for years. It adds wonder, warmth, and a touch of real magic to the experience. Best of all, it takes just a few minutes to create and costs absolutely nothing. Below you will find our free template, tips for making it unforgettable, and a bonus template for older kids.

What to Include in Your Tooth Fairy Letter

A great Tooth Fairy letter feels personal and real. Here are the key ingredients that make it sparkle:

  • Your child's name — Nothing makes a letter feel more real than seeing their own name written in the Tooth Fairy's handwriting.
  • Which tooth it was — Mention the specific tooth ("your front tooth" or "that wiggly bottom tooth") to show the Tooth Fairy was really paying attention.
  • Praise and encouragement — Celebrate their bravery. "I heard you were so brave when it came out!" goes a long way.
  • A sprinkle of magic — Reference fairy dust, a moonlit flight, or a tiny fairy door. The more details, the more believable it becomes.
  • A gentle reminder — Encourage brushing and taking care of their remaining teeth. The Tooth Fairy cares about dental hygiene!

Free Tooth Fairy Letter Template

Copy this template, fill in the blanks, and leave it under your child's pillow tonight. Print it on nice paper or hand-write it in your tiniest, most magical handwriting.

Dear [Child's Name],

I visited your pillow last night and found the most beautiful little tooth! It was so shiny and white — I could tell you have been doing a wonderful job brushing.

I flew all the way from Fairyland on my sparkly wings just to collect it. Your tooth will have a very special place in the Tooth Fairy Castle, right next to the other treasures from the bravest boys and girls.

I heard you were so brave when your [front tooth / bottom tooth / wiggly tooth] came out. That makes me so proud! Keep taking great care of your smile, and I will be back the next time you have a tooth ready for me.

Until then, look for a little sprinkle of fairy dust on your windowsill — that is how you will know I was really here.

With love and fairy dust,
The Tooth Fairy

Pro tip: Write the letter using your non-dominant hand or a calligraphy pen to create handwriting your child will not recognize as yours!

Tips to Make It Extra Special

The letter itself is magical, but a few extra touches can take it from special to absolutely unforgettable:

  • Add real glitter. Sprinkle a tiny bit of loose glitter inside the envelope or on the letter itself. When your child opens it, they will find actual "fairy dust."
  • Use tiny handwriting. The Tooth Fairy is small, so her handwriting should be too! Write as small and delicate as you can. Bonus points for using a sparkly gel pen.
  • Create a special envelope. Use a small envelope (a coin envelope works perfectly) and decorate it with a star sticker or a tiny wax seal. Write your child's name on the front in fancy script.
  • Leave a fairy dust trail. Sprinkle a trail of glitter from the window to your child's pillow. In the morning, they will see the exact path the Tooth Fairy took.
  • Include a tiny gift. A golden coin, a small charm, or a few dollars tucked inside the letter adds an extra layer of excitement.

Bonus: "Big Kid" Tooth Fairy Letter Template

As your child grows, the Tooth Fairy's letters should grow with them. This template works great for kids who have lost a few teeth already and are starting to feel like seasoned pros:

Dear [Child's Name],

Well, well, well — another tooth! You are becoming quite the expert at this. I have to say, this one was especially impressive. Strong roots, beautiful enamel — clearly the work of someone who takes brushing very seriously.

This is tooth number [number] in my collection from you, and each one has earned a special spot in the Fairy Vault. Not every kid gets a spot in the Vault, you know — only the truly brave ones.

I left you a little something as a thank you. Keep up the great work, keep smiling that amazing smile, and I will see you next time.

High fives and fairy dust,
The Tooth Fairy

P.S. — Tell your [mom/dad] I said thanks for keeping the path to your pillow clear. Last time I tripped over a [toy/shoe/stuffed animal] on my way in!

The P.S. at the end is a fun touch that makes older kids laugh and adds a sense of personality to the Tooth Fairy. Feel free to customize the details to reference something specific to your child's life — the more personal, the more magical.

Make Every Tooth Count

Your child will lose about 20 baby teeth between the ages of 6 and 12. That is 20 opportunities to create a moment of wonder, 20 letters to write, and 20 memories to treasure. You do not need to go big every time — some visits might be just a coin and a quick note, while others (like the very first tooth) deserve the full fairy treatment.

The key is consistency. When the Tooth Fairy always shows up, always leaves a note, and always makes it feel special, you are building a tradition that your child will carry with them long after the last tooth is gone. And someday, they might just write those same letters for their own kids.
